Kids' Pulse

The First Ever Children's Congress 2024

by Raynevaer A. Ambasing

On the 29th of November 2024, the Children's Congress
event was held at Barangay Quezon, Solano Nueva Vizcaya. Participated by the schools in
Barangay Quezon such as Kids Workshop Learning Center, TAPS Private School,
Solano West Elementary School, and Blest Christian School.

The program started with a prayer by Kids Workshop Learning Center’s
KPG President Mark Roland S. Dela Cruz,the singing of the National Anthem by BLEST’s student President Jamillah Leila Elbendari,
the Solano March was conducted by Solano West Elementary School’s SELG Janine B. Camangian, and the Panatang Makabata by Taps’
SELG President Jade Ysabel Obra. Welcome message by Hon. Marilyn V. Sultan, and the roll call of attendance by the event facililator.
State of Children Address was delivered by Hon. Eduardo DL. Tiongson, and lastly the message of support by Mayor Philip Agraan Dacayo.

The Children’s Congress program was a series of lectures and
lessons. First lecture was about the rights of children and was conducted by Mdm. Frances Grace P. Gonzales.
We learned about how and what to eat in a proper meal like go, grow, glow. It was conducted by Sheena Mae G.
Baraceros. The last lecture was about the types of abuses like sexual, verbal abuse, etc. We also learned
some Republic Acts protecting children from such abuses. This was conducted by Mdm. Reina Faye D. Litawan,
RSW

The event also included some fun games, raffles and team games.
The marshmallow tower was about making a tower with marshmallows and sticks with a team of 4.
Balloon relay was played by 10 people which was about passing the balloon to your teammates,
and the last was Draw Me. Hyannica Joy B. Calucin and Cleajohn Beatriz J. Mendiguarin both
pupils of Kids Workshop Learning Center bagged 1st place. It was about envisioning Barangay
Quezon in 5 years. The program ended with the closing remarks by Richard A. Ramos, the chairman
on Committee on Education.

Overall, the event was educational, fun, and we all enjoyed the first ever Children’s Congress Program!
